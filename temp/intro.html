Welcome to the 2017 edition of Endnotes, the conference hosted annually by the graduate students of the Department of English at the University of British Columbia. This year’s theme, “On the Edge: Borders, Boundaries, and Thresholds in an Age of Unease,” invites presenters and attendees to consider the many material and cultural processes of limit-making and limit-crossing that operate in the spaces we inhabit as humanities scholars, and as humans. Our choice of this theme was a response to  our own Age of Unease, with its ever-more-dizzying array of ruptures, retrenchments, and realignments, and yet the diversity of historical, geographical, and disciplinary perspectives represented in the conference program demonstrates that this concern is anything but unique to the here and now.

The conference will therefore be a wide-ranging exercise in what Derrida calls “limitrophy,” encouraging us to engage with “what sprouts or grows at the limit, around the limit, by maintaining the limit, but also what feeds the limit, generates it, raises it, and complicates it.” If we find ourselves occasionally feeling a bit precarious as we embark on this strange cultivation, that is because each limit, as Derrida reminds us, opens onto an abyss. Thank you for joining us in vertigo.
